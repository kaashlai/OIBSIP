Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as APJ Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and rose from humble beginnings to become a prominent aerospace engineer, credited with playing a key role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts.

Dr. Kalam was known as the "People's President" due to his deep connection with the youth of India and his efforts to inspire them to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.


"Dream is not that which you see while sleeping it is something that does not let you sleep."
- Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

He authored several books on topics ranging from nuclear physics to spirituality and received numerous awards and honors for his work.

He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997 for his contributions to the scientific community and his role in the country's nuclear capabilities.

He believed that young people were the key to India's future and worked tirelessly to inspire and empower them to pursue their dreams and contribute to the country's growth.

Throughout his life, he remained committed to education, science, and technology, advocating for the importance of innovation and creativity in nation-building.

"All Birds find shelter during a rain.But Eagle avoides rain by flying high above the Clouds. Problems are common, but attitude makes the difference!!!"
- Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to inspire millions around the world.

Dr. Kalam's life and work serve as a reminder of the power of dreams, the importance of hard work, and the potential for each individual to make a positive impact on the world.

This tribute page is dedicated to honoring his contributions and remembering his remarkable life.
